Projects

Implemented systems

Subsenate

GitHub →

A zkSNARK-based e-voting system that randomly selects a community of judges from a pool of eligible voters — cryptographic sortition. Using zero-knowledge proofs, the selection is verifiably random and private: nobody can predict or manipulate who gets chosen, yet anyone can verify the result is fair.

Sortition — random selection of citizen decision-makers — powered Athenian democracy. Subsenate brings it into the 21st century with ZK proofs.

zkSNARKs e-voting Sortition circom

Coercion-Resistant Elections

Try it → GitHub →

A full Rust implementation of coercion-resistant e-voting based on the JCJ protocol and Civitas. Implements the complete cryptographic core: DVRP credential issuance, threshold ElGamal key generation, PET-based duplicate and invalid-credential elimination, a two-pass RPC MixNet with public verifiability, and distributed decryption — all wired into a runnable election pipeline with a web UI.

The key insight of JCJ: a coercer can force you to vote under threat, but cannot prevent you from casting a silent override later. Your real vote always wins.

On Democracy

Values & conviction
"The cure for the ills of democracy is more democracy."

I believe that democracy is not just a political system — it is a living commitment to the idea that every person has an equal stake in the decisions that shape their life. That commitment is fragile, and it requires active work to sustain and deepen.

My technical research often intersects with democratic ideals. I have built two working voting systems: CRelections, a full implementation of coercion-resistant e-voting where your real vote always wins even under physical duress, and Subsenate, a zkSNARK-based sortition system that randomly selects a community of judges the way ancient Athens did — except with cryptographic proofs. These are not papers. They run. I am drawn to these problems precisely because they are not purely technical — they are deeply political.

I am particularly interested in institutional design: how constitutions, voting rules, and deliberative processes can be reformed to be more representative, more resistant to capture, and more capable of handling the complexity of modern governance. I believe technology can help — but only if it is designed with democratic values at its core, not bolted on as an afterthought.
